The Way Back

2020 Directed by Gavin O'Connor

Synopsis

Every loss is another fight.

A former basketball all-star, who has lost his wife and family foundation in a struggle with addiction attempts to regain his soul and salvation by becoming the coach of a disparate ethnically mixed high school basketball team at his alma mater.

  • Review by ryanmovieguy ★★★

    This movie is hard not to like, in general it is a very pleasant watch. The reason for this is because if feels like every sports movie ever. There are elements here that appear to force in cliches for no reason other than to adhere to standard genre tropes. That being said the formula works, the characters are all underdogs and the film gives them plenty of charisma.

    The true highlight here is Ben Affleck, who gives possibly the best performance of his career. He is clearly pulling from some of his personal struggles with alcoholism and channels that into a performance that is very raw. There is clear pain that is behind his character and the film and his performance do a stellar job at not embellishing it.

    Without Affleck this film would be a completely forgettable run of the mill sports movie. He truly elevates the material and he alone makes this movie worth watching.
